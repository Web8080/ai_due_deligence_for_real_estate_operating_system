# Financial model assumptions

**Author:** Victor.I

This document explains the numbers embedded in `reos_financial_model.xlsx` (generated by `scripts/build_financial_model.py`). **All figures are illustrative** for an enterprise / proptech SaaS shaped like an operating system for real estate (e.g. REOS). Replace with your fundraise deck, contracts, and hiring plan.

---

## What the model is / is not

| Included | Not included |
|----------|----------------|
| Monthly MRR and simple revenue = MRR | Deferred revenue, multi-year contracts, ramp deals |
| COGS as a flat % of revenue | Channel margin, pass-through data fees broken out |
| Payroll as base + step-ups every 6 months | Individual headcount roster, bonus pools, equity |
| S&M as % of revenue | Pipeline, CAC payback, funnel conversion |
| Single one-time cash hit in month 1 | Cap table, debt, interest, taxes |
| Ending cash = prior cash + monthly net | Working capital, AR collections delay |

**Net operating cash** is a **rough** check: can the burn and growth assumptions live inside starting cash over 36 months? It is **not** a substitute for audited forecasts.

## Line-by-line assumptions

### Starting cash ($2,500,000)

**Meaning:** Cash on hand at month 1 before that month’s operations.  
**Assumption:** A seed or small Series A sized for ~18–30 months of runway in a conservative case, depending on growth.  
**Risk:** If enterprise sales cycles are long, you burn cash before MRR catches up; starting cash may be too low.  
**Validate:** Against actual bank statement and committed equity.

### Initial MRR ($18,000 / month)

**Meaning:** Recurring revenue at the start of the model (annualize ≈ $216k).  
**Assumption:** A small set of design partners or early tenants on annual contracts, not a greenfield $0 business.  
**Risk:** If you are pre-revenue, set to **0** and fund a longer pre-revenue phase explicitly.  
**Validate:** Sum of signed contract values / 12, appropriately weighted for ramp.

### MRR growth (7.5% months 1–12, 4.5% months 13–24, 2.5% months 25–36)

**Meaning:** Month-over-month growth of MRR within each band.  
**Assumption:** Front-loaded growth while the product and GTM mature, then natural deceleration as the base grows (law of large numbers).  
**Risk:** 7.5% MoM is aggressive (~138% annualized if sustained); the step-down is meant to reflect realism. Missing quarters resets the whole runway.  
**Validate:** Against historical logo count, expansion revenue, and churn.

### COGS (16% of revenue)

**Meaning:** Variable cost to serve revenue: hosting, third-party APIs, customer support load, **allocated** professional services variable component.  
**Assumption:** SaaS-like gross margins in the low–mid 80s after variable costs.  
**Risk:** Heavy manual onboarding or data vendor minimums can push this higher.  
**Validate:** Actual cloud bills + support FTE loaded into variable where possible.

### Base payroll ($195,000 / month)

**Meaning:** All people-related cash cost per month **salary + benefits + payroll taxes** rolled into one number (not headcount detail).  
**Assumption:** Roughly a lean team across eng, product, CS, and partial GTM in a high-cost market (or remote mix).  
**Risk:** Does not model raises, attrition backfill, or sales commission spikes.  
**Validate:** HRIS or finance rollup; replace with row-per-role when serious.

### Payroll step (+$22,000 every 6 months)

**Meaning:** Permanent increase to monthly payroll at month 7, 13, 19, etc.  
**Assumption:** One incremental hire or contractor bundle twice a year as revenue scales.  
**Risk:** Step function understates continuous hiring; smooth to monthly adds if needed.  
**Validate:** Hiring plan keyed to revenue milestones.

### Infra & tools ($14,000 / month)

**Meaning:** Cloud, CI, observability, CRM, productivity seats, security tools.  
**Assumption:** Moderate enterprise stack; scales weakly with usage in this simple model.  
**Risk:** LLM / vector spend tied to usage can explode; model as variable % or separate line.  
**Validate:** Invoices from cloud + key vendors.

### G&A ($42,000 / month)

**Meaning:** Finance, HR, insurance, legal retainer, accounting, small office (if any).  
**Assumption:** Fractional roles early, more fixed than revenue in the near term.  
**Risk:** Litigation or compliance (SOC2, HIPAA, etc.) can be lumpy; month-1 one-time captures **some** of that, not all.  
**Validate:** Actual contracts.

### S&M (22% of revenue)

**Meaning:** Sales and marketing **cash** as a percentage of that month’s revenue.  
**Assumption:** Land-and-expand enterprise motion: spend rises with **recognized** revenue (which is simplified here as MRR).  
**Risk:** Classic chicken-and-egg: you may need marketing **before** revenue; this % model understates early GTM if MRR is tiny. Consider a **minimum monthly S&M floor** in a v2 model.  
**Validate:** Marketing actuals + sales comp plans.

### One-time month 1 ($95,000)

**Meaning:** Cash out the door in month 1 for legal formation, security audits, initial customer implementation support, or similar.  
**Assumption:** Lumpy spend concentrated at company formation or major compliance push.  
**Risk:** Real SOC2 / external counsel can exceed this; adjust or spread across months.  
**Validate:** Vendor quotes and legal invoices.

## Runway

**Definition here:** Months of projection until **ending cash ≤ 0** under the monthly net series, **starting from the opening balance**. If cash never goes negative within 36 months, the worksheet states that runway extends past the horizon (under these drivers only).

**Caveats:** No fundraising injects cash mid-horizon; no revenue quality issues (churn, bad debt). Add a **Financing** row to the monthly sheet when you model a raise.
