# Problem-killing product lens (REOS)

**Purpose:** Every surface should answer ``does this reduce time'' or ``does this reduce risk''. Capabilities that do not map to those are backlog noise until wired into a pipeline.

## Non-negotiables

- **Decision output:** Verdict (recommend / hold / reject scaffold), confidence band, stated assumptions, downside case. Committee and policy stay the human gate; the system proposes and explains.
- **Workflow as first-class:** Stage changes, follow-ups, tasks, and CRM updates should be expressed as orchestration, not as a generic ``we have AI'' bullet.
- **Pipelines not features:** Document upload is not the product; ``extracted, validated, tied to model, impacts decision'' is.
- **Measurable KPIs:** Time to evaluate, time to diligence sign-off, dwell in investment committee. If it is not in the product data model, the promise is easy to dismiss.

## Layers (narrative alignment)

1. **Deal intelligence:** Ends in recommendation + assumptions + risks, not only charts.
2. **Document intelligence:** Due diligence automation (gaps, inconsistencies, tie-out to underwriting).
3. **Workflow automation:** Who does what next, auto progression where policy allows, reminders and assignments.
4. **Decision + execution engine (bounded):** Suggested actions and stage moves with audit trail; destructive sends and capital actions remain gated.

## Deal workspace

The deal page is a **live decision surface**: current verdict, top risks, blocking data, next best actions. Passive tabs without that framing read as another data room skin.

## Investors

Tracking alone is not enough. Surface **who needs follow-up**, **likely ghosting**, and **suggested next message** (rules or ML later), always with human approval for outbound comms in regulated contexts.

## Financial narrative

Tie unit economics to automation: analyst hours per deal, deals per month, conversion deltas. Generic runway charts without those levers undersell the thesis.

## Honesty line for stakeholders

Heuristic verdicts and hints are **draft aids**. Legal, IC, and firm policy own the final call; the value is speed to a structured decision and fewer dropped threads, not autopilot capital deployment.
